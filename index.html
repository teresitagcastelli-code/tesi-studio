Process

Slow work,
in small rooms.

Each piece begins with a thread and a drawing. The miniatures are embroidered by hand on cotton or silk, sometimes barely two centimeters across. Once finished, they're mounted on gold-leafed panels and sealed inside a custom-cut acrylic box.

Nothing is printed. Nothing is batched. A single work takes between two weeks and three months to finish.

About

Teresita Castelli

I'm an artist from Argentina. I trained as a textile artist and spent years looking for a way to make paintings you could walk around, things that felt less like pictures and more like small worlds you stumbled on by accident.

I found it in embroidery. The miniatures I stitch feel like characters to me: tiny garments, tiny offerings, tiny strangers. The acrylic box is the room they live in. Gold leaf is the light.

Every piece is made by my own hands in my Buenos Aires studio. I take a small number of commissions a year, and I ship anywhere in the world.
